## Brand Voice & Tone Guidelines

Aequitas Index speaks to professionals who distrust noise and reward rigor. The tone is:

- **Declarative, not explanatory.** State findings. Let the data argue for itself.
- **Confident, not evangelical.** No mission-speak. No buzzwords. Numbers first.
- **Precise language.** "Efficiency delta," "overperformer watchlist," "input/output divergence" — use the vocabulary of the product.
- **Respect the reader's intelligence.** The audience manages sovereign risk models and capital allocation mandates. Write accordingly.

Reference register: Bloomberg Terminal documentation, IMF working paper abstracts, CFA Institute research briefs.

---

## Core Data Hooks

Aequitas Index delivers five key data-driven insights that correct the GII's limitations:

1. **The Outlier Hook — "The Hidden Overperformer"**  
   Compute the Input/Output Efficiency Score for any country, revealing how it punches above its weight. Example: "Kenya produces 2.3x the innovation output its institutional inputs would predict — ranking it #4 globally on efficiency, despite a raw GII rank of #89."

2. **The Peer Hook — "Your True Comparables"**  
   Reframe rankings within income-group peers instead of global absolutes. Example: "Among lower-middle-income Sub-Saharan economies, Kenya ranks #1 in innovation efficiency — ahead of Ghana, Tanzania, and Ethiopia."

3. **The Trend Hook — "The Trajectory Signal"**  
   Track efficiency scores over time to identify inflection points. Example: "Vietnam's efficiency score has increased 34% over five years, crossing into 'Emerging Overperformer' territory in 2019."

4. **The Geospatial Hook — "The Regional Cluster"**  
   Map regional innovation clusters obscured by raw GII scores. Example: East Africa shows a concentration of efficiency overperformers not visible in absolute rankings.

5. **The Digital Twin Hook — "The Predictive Peer"**  
   Identify developmental analogues using historical panel data. Example: "Based on its current trajectory and peer patterns, this country has an 18-month window before its efficiency premium gets priced in."

### `/methodology` — Methodology

**Page Headline:** `The adjustment model. Documented. Auditable. Open.`

**Subheadline:** Aequitas Index does not replace the GII. It corrects it. Here is exactly how.

---

**Section 1: Why the GII Understates Emerging Market Innovation**
Brief, clinical explanation of three structural biases:
- **PCT patent overreliance:** PCT filings systematically favor economies with resources to navigate international IP regimes — not economies with the most innovation activity.
- **ICT-heavy indicator weighting:** Formal broadband and ICT infrastructure metrics miss mobile-first, informal digital innovation concentrated in Sub-Saharan Africa and Southeast Asia.
- **Income-blind cross-country comparison:** Ranking Rwanda against Switzerland on the same absolute scale produces a signal that is worse than useless for capital allocation purposes.

*This is not a critique of WIPO. It is an acknowledgment of what the GII was designed to do — and what it was not.*

---

**Section 2: The Five Dataset Criteria**
How Aequitas selects and validates every data source:

1. **GII Core Indicators** — All 81 indicators ingested at pillar level; Input Sub-Index and Output Sub-Index treated separately; historical panel 2011–present.
2. **Peer-Group Context** — World Bank income classification normalization; GDP per capita (PPP), HDI, urbanization as context overlays.
3. **Alternative Innovation Indicators** — Mobile money penetration (GSMA), VC deal count (Crunchbase/PitchBook), GitHub contributions per capita, tech-skill migration (LinkedIn Economic Graph), startup formation rates.
4. **Patent & IP Correction** — Utility model filings alongside PCT; patent intensity relative to R&D expenditure, not absolute filing counts.
5. **Data Quality & Governance** — 35% missing-data exclusion threshold; vintage flags on stale indicators; anti-collinearity management; annual external benchmark validation.

---

**Section 3: Transparency Commitment**
- Full methodology documentation published in plain language
- Adjustment logic open-sourced where possible
- Annual peer review through academic partnership program
